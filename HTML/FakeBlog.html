Vector Arithmetic

A vector is made of either two or three components, denoted as x and y, or x, y, and z. When you add or subtract two vectors, you get a new vector as the answer. The components of one vector is added or subtracted from the components of the other vector resulting in a new vector.

The following table shows Vector A and Vector B added together resulting in Vector C. Simply add the x components of Vector A and B to get the x comonent of C, same for the y components.

Vector X Y
Vector A 5 -3
Vector B 4 6
Vector C 9 3
